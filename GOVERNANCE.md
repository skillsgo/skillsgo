# SkillsGo Governance

SkillsGo currently uses a maintainer-led governance model. The repository owner is the final decision maker for releases, security response, contributor access, and changes to public product or protocol contracts.

## Decision Process

- Routine implementation decisions are made through issue and pull-request review.
- Changes to public protocols, persisted formats, identity, security boundaries, compatibility, or cross-context ownership require explicit maintainer agreement and may require an Architecture Decision Record.
- Accepted architectural decisions live in `docs/adr/`; domain language and ownership live in the applicable context and GEB maps.
- Lack of immediate maintainer response is not acceptance. Contributors should avoid investing in large changes before direction is confirmed.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are expected to:

- apply the Code of Conduct consistently;
- review contributions in good faith;
- protect credentials and private vulnerability reports;
- keep releases and compatibility claims accurate;
- disclose conflicts of interest that materially affect a decision;
- grant elevated repository access conservatively.

## Becoming a Maintainer

Sustained contributors may be invited to maintain an area after demonstrating sound technical judgment, respectful collaboration, reliable reviews, and an understanding of the relevant domain boundaries. Access may be reduced or removed for inactivity, security reasons, or repeated failure to meet these responsibilities.

This governance model may evolve as the maintainer group and community grow. Material governance changes are reviewed publicly through a pull request.
